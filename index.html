Underlying factors

What is it that really causes communication between people to fail and what effects does it have? Ambiguity, loss of prestige or a perceived lack of respect are common factors that can contribute to conflicts between people. Stress, peer pressure, balance of power, drugs and mental illness are some of the underlying reasons why people sometimes do not behave as we expected. Worry about being exposed to threats and violence contributes to stress and poorer job satisfaction. What consequences could an unexpected incident with a troublesome person have for your staff, and in the long run for the brand?

Important tools

How well prepared are your staff to deal with upset, affected or aggressive people in their daily activities? In all professions where there is one or more interfaces with customers, clients or guests, it is crucial to have solid knowledge of interpersonal interaction and tools for conflict management. Learning to prevent and manage conflict-filled situations is also an important part of every workplace's systematic work environment and safety work. Learn the techniques to deal effectively with people who do not behave as we expected, e.g. upset customers, threatening clients or self-absorbed visitors. Having tools and knowledge reduces the risk of ill health, sick leave and staff turnover.

Services

Breakfast lectures

Some of the most troublesome personality types that occur in the workplace are narcissists and psychopaths. They create friction in the staff group and in the worst case damage both the company's reputation and the employees' trust. The lecture contains a review of the manipulator's special characteristics and how you maximize your chances of discovering them before you hire and how to best relate to a manipulative and self-absorbed personality type.

Workshops

Practical workshop, half day. Communication in case of conflict. To say no and reject. The reptile, the monkey and the human - our three-part brain. Manage your own and others' reactions. Practical workshop, full day. In addition to the content of the workshop half day, also: Physical self-defense. Practical exercises and strategies for dealing with a threatening or violent situation. Preventive screening of the workplace, in order to minimize potential conflict areas.

Unstoppable! - Full-day training

A full day filled with practical exercises in self-defense. Scenario training. The different forms of violence. The training takes place in a small group, in a safe environment. No previous knowledge required.

One-to-one

Coaching and guiding conversations based on your individual needs. Examples of work areas: setting boundaries and saying no, dealing with threats, resolving group conflicts, difficult conversations, etc.
